FAQ

Questions we hear every day.

Straight answers — no runaround.

A primary tradeline is a credit account that is opened in your name and reports directly to the credit bureaus as your own account. It shows up on your credit report with payment history, credit limit, and account age — all of which factor into your score. This is different from an authorized user tradeline, which is simply being added to someone else's account.
Most tradelines post within 4–6 weeks of submission to the bureaus. We submit to the bureaus within 5 business days of confirmed payment and document verification. If your tradeline hasn't posted within 60 days, you receive a full refund — that's our guarantee.
Results vary by credit profile. A tradeline adds a positive account with clean payment history and available credit, which typically benefits score factors like payment history and utilization. However, if you have significant negative items or other issues on your profile, the impact may be limited. This is exactly why we recommend a strategy session first — so you understand what will and won't move your specific score.
CPNs (Credit Privacy Numbers) are not a legal alternative to a Social Security Number for credit purposes. Using a CPN on a credit application is considered fraud under federal law. AFS only works with valid SSNs to protect both our clients and our business from legal exposure. Any application submitted with a CPN results in immediate forfeiture of payment with no refund.
